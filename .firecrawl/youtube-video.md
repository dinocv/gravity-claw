Key Points

* An AI agent (Larry) built on OpenClaw autonomously creates TikTok slideshows, reads analytics, and iterates on content—driving hundreds of dollars in MRR with almost zero manual effort.

* The “Larry Loop” is a full-funnel feedback cycle: TikTok analytics feed into content creation, and app metrics feed back into the top of the funnel so the agent continuously improves.

* Posting TikTok content as a draft (rather than directly via API) lets you add trending sounds and avoids the algorithm penalty for bot-posted content.

* Hooks drive views; CTAs drive conversions. Diagnosing which is underperforming is the key to scaling.

* OpenClaw skills are locally owned, fully editable, and free from hosting or subscription costs—Oliver argues they will change how we think about SaaS.

* Picking a model (Claude or OpenAI) matters far less than learning how to work with it; 98% of users will see little difference between incremental model upgrades.

Section Summaries

1. Early Content Experiments and the Shift to AI Employees

Oliver tried manual hook-and-demo videos, a SaaS scheduling tool, and Canva slideshows before discovering OpenClaw. I highlight the broader mindset shift: instead of buying a tool to automate a function, you spin up an AI employee with a single job. That reframe led Oliver to create Larry.

2. Iterating to Virality: Image Models, Hooks, and the First 137K-View Post

Larry’s first posts flopped—AI-generated faces looked uncanny, formats were wrong, and engagement was low. But by combining a winning image model with a proven hook, they hit 137,000 views. Oliver explains how Larry uses TikTok analytics to trace back to previous winners and remix them.

3. The Larry Loop: A Full-Funnel Feedback Engine

Oliver introduces the Larry Loop: TikTok analytics feed into content creation, and app-level metrics (downloads, subscriptions, churn) feed back into the top of the funnel. This loop is platform-agnostic—you can plug in video generation, drive traffic to a website, or sell a physical product.
